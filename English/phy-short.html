Physics - Short Answer Questions

Class 12 Bihar Board | Important Questions & Answers (English Medium) | 2 Marks Each
Q1. Write the Boolean expressions for AND, OR, NOT, NAND, and NOR gates.
Ans: The Boolean expressions for various logic gates are:
  • AND Gate: Y = A ċ B
  • OR Gate: Y = A + B
  • NOT Gate: Y = Ā (A bar)
  • NAND Gate: Y = A ċ B (Combination of AND and NOT).
  • NOR Gate: Y = A + B (Combination of OR and NOT).
Q2. State Lenz’s law of electromagnetic induction. How is it related to the conservation of energy?
Ans: Lenz’s Law: It states that the direction of the induced EMF and induced current is such that it always opposes the cause (change in magnetic flux) that produces it.
Conservation of Energy: The mechanical work done in moving the magnet against this opposing force is converted into electrical energy. Thus, it obeys the law of conservation of energy.
Q3. What is the principle of a transformer? Mention the energy losses in it.
Ans: A transformer works on the principle of Mutual Induction. It transfers electrical energy from one circuit to another without changing the frequency.
Energy Losses:
  1. Copper Loss: Heat loss due to resistance of coils.
  2. Eddy Current Loss: Heat loss in the iron core due to induced currents.
  3. Hysteresis Loss: Energy loss due to repeated magnetization and demagnetization of the core.
Q4. Convert decimal 25 to binary, and binary (1101)2 to decimal.
Ans: 1. 25 to Binary: Dividing 25 by 2 repeatedly and recording remainders gives (11001)2.
2. (1101)2 to Decimal:
1×23 + 1×22 + 0×21 + 1×20
= 8 + 4 + 0 + 1 = 13
Q5. What is total internal reflection? Write its conditions. Define critical angle.
Ans: Total Internal Reflection (TIR): When a light ray traveling from a denser to a rarer medium hits the interface at an angle greater than the critical angle, it reflects back into the denser medium.
Conditions: 1. Light must travel from denser to rarer medium. 2. Angle of incidence > Critical angle.
Critical Angle: The angle of incidence in the denser medium for which the angle of refraction in the rarer medium becomes 90°.
Q6. Define electric flux and write Gauss’s Law.
Ans: Electric Flux (φ): The total number of electric field lines passing normally through a given surface. Its SI unit is N m2 C-1.
Gauss’s Law: The total electric flux through a closed surface in vacuum is equal to 1/ε0 times the net charge (q) enclosed by the surface.
φ = ∫ EċdA = q / ε0
Q7. Define drift velocity. Write the relation between electric current and drift velocity.
Ans: Drift Velocity (vd): The average velocity with which free electrons in a conductor get drifted towards the positive end under the influence of an external electric field. It is of the order of 10-4 m/s.
Relation: I = n A e vd (Where I = Current, n = electron density, A = Area).
Q8. Write two properties of electromagnetic waves.
Ans: Two properties of Electromagnetic (EM) waves are:
  1. They are transverse in nature, meaning electric and magnetic fields oscillate perpendicular to the direction of propagation.
  2. They do not require any material medium for propagation and travel with the speed of light (c = 3 × 108 m/s) in a vacuum.
Q9. Write two shortcomings of Bohr’s atomic model.
Ans: Limitations of Bohr's Model:
  1. It could only explain the spectrum of single-electron atoms (like Hydrogen) but failed for complex multi-electron atoms.
  2. It could not explain the splitting of spectral lines under magnetic fields (Zeeman effect) and electric fields (Stark effect).
Q10. Define work function and threshold frequency.
Ans:
  • Work Function (φ0): The minimum amount of energy required by an electron to just escape from a metal surface at zero Kelvin.
  • Threshold Frequency (ν0): The minimum frequency of incident light below which photoelectric emission is not possible, irrespective of the intensity of light.
Q11. Define Angle of Dip and Magnetic Declination.
Ans:
  • Angle of Dip (Inclination): The angle made by the earth's total magnetic field with the horizontal direction at any place. It is 90° at poles and 0° at the equator.
  • Magnetic Declination: The angle between the geographic meridian and the magnetic meridian at a given place.
Q12. What is a shunt? Write its two uses.
Ans: A shunt is a low resistance wire connected in parallel to a galvanometer to provide an alternative path for excess current.
Uses: 1. To convert a galvanometer into an ammeter. 2. To protect the galvanometer coil from damage due to strong currents.
Q13. Define electric dipole moment. Write the formula for torque on a dipole in a uniform field.
Ans: Electric Dipole Moment (p): It is defined as the product of the magnitude of one of the charges (q) and the distance between them (2l).
p = q × 2l
It is a vector quantity directed from negative to positive charge.
Torque: τ = pE sinθ (or vector τ = p × E).
Q14. State the law of radioactive decay. Define half-life.
Ans: Decay Law: The rate of disintegration of radioactive atoms at any instant is directly proportional to the number of undecayed atoms present at that instant.
dN/dt ∝ -N ⇒ N = N0 e-λt
Half-life (T1/2): The time interval in which half of the original radioactive nuclei disintegrate. T1/2 = 0.693 / λ.
Q15. Write the necessary conditions for interference of light.
Ans: Conditions for interference:
  1. The two light sources must be coherent, i.e., they must maintain a constant phase difference.
  2. The sources should be monochromatic (single wavelength).
  3. Constructive Interference: Path difference = nλ.
  4. Destructive Interference: Path difference = (2n+1)λ/2.
Q16. What is modulation? Mention its types.
Ans: Modulation: The process of superimposing a low-frequency audio signal (information) onto a high-frequency carrier wave for transmission over long distances.
Types: 1. Amplitude Modulation (AM) 2. Frequency Modulation (FM) 3. Phase Modulation (PM).
Q17. What is the principle of a potentiometer? Write two uses.
Ans: Principle: When a constant current flows through a wire of uniform cross-section, the potential drop (V) across any length (l) of the wire is directly proportional to that length (V ∝ l).
Uses: 1. To compare the EMFs of two primary cells. 2. To measure the internal resistance of a cell.
Q18. State Biot-Savart’s law.
Ans: Biot-Savart’s law gives the magnetic field (dB) due to a small current element (dl) carrying current (I) at a distance (r):
dB = (μ0 / 4π) × (I dl sinθ / r2)
Where θ is the angle between the current element and the position vector.
Q19. What are eddy currents? Write two applications.
Ans: Eddy Currents: The circulating induced currents produced in a bulk piece of conductor when the magnetic flux linked with it changes.
Applications: 1. Induction Furnace: To melt metals using heat produced by eddy currents. 2. Magnetic Brakes: Used in electric trains to stop them smoothly.
Q20. Distinguish between n-type and p-type semiconductors.
Ans:
  • n-type: Formed by doping a pure semiconductor with a Pentavalent impurity (e.g., Arsenic). The majority charge carriers are electrons.
  • p-type: Formed by doping with a Trivalent impurity (e.g., Boron). The majority charge carriers are holes.
Q21. Differentiate between diamagnetic, paramagnetic, and ferromagnetic substances.
Ans:
  • Diamagnetic: Weakly repelled by magnets (e.g., Water, Copper).
  • Paramagnetic: Weakly attracted by magnets (e.g., Aluminum, Oxygen).
  • Ferromagnetic: Strongly attracted by magnets (e.g., Iron, Nickel).
Q22. What is Lorentz force? Write the expression.
Ans: The total force experienced by a charged particle (q) moving with velocity (v) in a region where both electric field (E) and magnetic field (B) are present is called Lorentz force.
F = Fe + Fm = qE + q(v × B)
Q23. On what factors does the capacitance of a parallel plate capacitor depend?
Ans: The capacitance is given by C = (K ε0 A) / d. It depends on:
1. Area of plates (A): Directly proportional (C ∝ A).
2. Distance between plates (d): Inversely proportional (C ∝ 1/d).
3. Dielectric constant (K): Increases with the introduction of a dielectric medium.
Q24. State Kirchhoff’s laws.
Ans:
1. Junction Law: The algebraic sum of currents meeting at any junction is zero (Σ I = 0). Based on conservation of charge.
2. Loop Law: The algebraic sum of changes in potential around any closed loop is zero (Σ V = 0). Based on conservation of energy.
Q25. State Brewster’s law of polarization.
Ans: Brewster’s Law: When unpolarized light is incident on a transparent medium at a specific angle called the polarizing angle (ip), the reflected light is completely plane-polarized.
μ = tan ip
Where μ is the refractive index of the medium.
Q26. Define RMS value and Peak value of AC. Write the relation.
Ans: RMS Value (Irms): The root mean square value of alternating current over one complete cycle. It represents the equivalent DC current that produces the same heat.
Peak Value (I0): The maximum value attained by the AC current.
Relation: Irms = I0 / √2 = 0.707 I0.
Q27. What is de Broglie hypothesis? Write the formula for wavelength.
Ans: de Broglie Hypothesis: It states that every moving material particle (like an electron) is associated with a wave nature, known as matter waves.
Wavelength (λ): Inversely proportional to momentum (p).
λ = h / p = h / mv
Where h is Planck’s constant.
Q28. Differentiate between primary and secondary rainbows.
Ans: Primary Rainbow: Formed by two refractions and one total internal reflection inside raindrops. Red is on the outer edge, and it is brighter.
Secondary Rainbow: Formed by two refractions and two total internal reflections. Violet is on the outer edge, and it is fainter than the primary rainbow.
Q29. Write two main characteristics of laser rays.
Ans: The main characteristics of LASER are:
  1. Monochromatic: Laser light consists of a single specific wavelength (color).
  2. Coherent: All the waves are in the same phase.
  3. Highly Directional: It travels long distances without spreading.
Q30. Write the expression for the energy stored in a charged capacitor.
Ans: The potential energy (U) stored in the electric field between the plates of a charged capacitor is given by:
U = 1/2 CV2 = Q2 / 2C = 1/2 QV
Where C is capacitance, V is potential difference, and Q is charge.
Q31. State Ampere’s circuital law.
Ans: Ampere’s Law: The line integral of the magnetic field (B) around any closed loop in a vacuum is equal to μ0 times the net current (I) enclosed by the loop.
∫ B ċ dl = μ0 I
Q32. Define self-inductance and mutual inductance.
Ans: Self-Inductance (L): The phenomenon of induction of EMF in a coil due to a change of current in the same coil.
Mutual Inductance (M): The phenomenon of induction of EMF in a coil due to a change of current in a neighboring coil.
The SI unit for both is Henry (H).
Q33. Define electric potential. What is an equipotential surface?
Ans: Electric Potential: Work done in bringing a unit positive charge from infinity to a point in the electric field.
Equipotential Surface: A surface where the electric potential is the same at all points.
Properties: Work done to move a charge on it is zero; Electric field lines are perpendicular to the surface.
Q34. Define wavefront. State Huygens’ principle.
Ans: Wavefront: The locus of all particles of a medium vibrating in the same phase at a given instant.
Huygens’ Principle: 1. Every point on a wavefront acts as a source of secondary wavelets. 2. These wavelets spread out in all directions with the speed of light.
Q35. Define inductive reactance, capacitive reactance, and impedance.
Ans: Reactance: Opposition offered by inductor (XL = ωL) or capacitor (XC = 1/ωC) to AC.
Impedance (Z): The total effective resistance offered by an LCR circuit containing resistance, inductance, and capacitance.
Z = √(R2 + (XL - XC)2)
Q36. Two thin lenses of power P1 and P2 are in contact. Find the combined focal length and power.
Ans: For two thin lenses in contact:
Combined Power (P): The algebraic sum of individual powers. P = P1 + P2.
Combined Focal Length (f):
1/f = 1/f1 + 1/f2
Q37. What are ohmic and non-ohmic resistances? Give examples.
Ans: Ohmic Resistances: Conductors that strictly obey Ohm’s law (V ∝ I) at constant temperature. Their V-I graph is linear. Example: Metals like Copper, Silver.
Non-Ohmic Resistances: Components that do not obey Ohm’s law. Their V-I graph is non-linear. Example: Diode, Transistor, Electrolyte.
Q38. Define dielectric strength and relative permittivity.
Ans: Dielectric Strength: The maximum electric field that a dielectric medium can withstand without breakdown (insulation failure).
Relative Permittivity (K or εr): The ratio of the permittivity of the medium (ε) to the permittivity of free space (ε0).
Q39. Explain the difference between nuclear fission and nuclear fusion.
Ans: Fission: The splitting of a heavy nucleus (like Uranium) into two lighter nuclei with the release of energy. (e.g., Nuclear Reactor).
Fusion: The combining of two light nuclei (like Hydrogen) to form a heavier nucleus, releasing tremendous energy. (e.g., Sun's energy).
Q40. Explain the terms: FAX, WWW, Sky Waves.
Ans: FAX: Facsimile telegraphy; electronic reproduction of documents at a distance.
WWW: World Wide Web; a system of interlinked documents accessed via the internet.
Sky Waves: Radio waves reflected by the ionosphere back to earth, used for long-distance communication.
Q41. What is a cyclotron? State its limitations.
Ans: Cyclotron: A device used to accelerate positively charged particles (protons, deuterons) to high energies.
Limitations: 1. It cannot accelerate uncharged particles like neutrons. 2. It cannot accelerate electrons effectively due to relativistic mass increase.
Q42. How do you identify the resistance of a carbon resistor using color code?
Ans: Using the formula: R = AB × 10C ± D%.
Colors correspond to numbers: Black(0), Brown(1), Red(2), Orange(3), Yellow(4), Green(5), Blue(6), Violet(7), Grey(8), White(9).
The 4th band is tolerance: Gold (±5%), Silver (±10%).
Q43. Define current density. Is it a scalar or a vector?
Ans: Current Density (J): The electric current flowing per unit cross-sectional area perpendicular to the direction of current flow.
J = I / A
It is a vector quantity, directed along the flow of positive charge. Unit: A/m2.
Q44. Define mobility of charge carriers.
Ans: Mobility (μ): The magnitude of drift velocity (vd) acquired by a charge carrier per unit electric field (E).
μ = vd / E
Its SI unit is m2 V-1 s-1.
Q45. Define magnetic moment. Write its SI unit and dimension.
Ans: Magnetic Moment (M): It measures the strength of a magnet or current loop.
Formula: M = NIA (Coil) or M = m × 2l (Bar magnet).
It is a vector quantity.
SI Unit: A m2 (Ampere-meter square).
Dimension: [AL2].
Q46. What is the Seebeck effect?
Ans: Seebeck Effect: When two dissimilar metals are joined to form a loop (thermocouple) and their junctions are kept at different temperatures, an electromotive force (EMF) is induced, causing current to flow. This is also called the thermoelectric effect.
Q47. Why does the sky appear blue / Why are danger signals red?
Ans: Due to Scattering of light (Rayleigh’s Law: Intensity ∝ 1/λ4).
1. Blue Sky: Blue has a shorter wavelength, so it scatters the most by atmospheric particles.
2. Red Signals: Red has the longest wavelength, so it scatters the least and can be seen from a long distance through fog/smoke.
Q48. What is an optical fiber? On what principle does it work?
Ans: Optical Fiber: A thin fiber made of glass or plastic used to transmit light signals over long distances with negligible energy loss.
Principle: It works on the principle of Total Internal Reflection (TIR). Light reflects repeatedly inside the core.
Q49. What is wattless current? Define power factor.
Ans: Wattless Current: In a purely inductive or capacitive circuit (R=0), current flows but the average power consumed is zero. Such current is called wattless current.
Power Factor (cosφ): The ratio of true power to apparent power in an AC circuit. cosφ = R/Z.
Q50. How does the resistivity of a conductor and a semiconductor vary with temperature?
Ans: 1. Conductors: Resistivity increases with temperature due to increased collision frequency (decreased relaxation time).
2. Semiconductors: Resistivity decreases exponentially with temperature because more charge carriers (electron-hole pairs) are generated.
Q51. Explain the working of an LED or Solar Cell.
Ans: LED: A forward-biased p-n junction diode that emits light when electrons and holes recombine, releasing energy as photons.
Solar Cell: An unbiased p-n junction that converts solar energy (light) directly into electrical energy based on the photovoltaic effect.
Q52. What is the difference between magnification and magnifying power?
Ans: Linear Magnification (m): Ratio of the size of the image to the size of the object (m = h'/h).
Magnifying Power (MP): Also called Angular Magnification. It is the ratio of the angle subtended by the image at the eye to the angle subtended by the object at the unaided eye.
Q53. Write two properties of alpha (α) and beta (β) rays.
Ans: Alpha (α) Rays: Helium nuclei (2He4). They have high ionizing power but low penetrating power.
Beta (β) Rays: Fast-moving electrons (e-). They have lower ionizing power than alpha but higher penetrating power (about 100 times more).
Q54. State Curie’s law in magnetism.
Ans: Curie’s Law: It states that the magnetic susceptibility (χm) of a paramagnetic material is inversely proportional to its absolute temperature (T).
χm ∝ 1/T
Thus, as temperature increases, magnetism decreases.
Q55. What do you mean by the resolving power of a microscope?
Ans: The ability of a microscope to form separate and distinct images of two very closely placed objects.
Formula: R.P. = 2μ sinθ / 1.22 λ
Resolving power increases if the wavelength (λ) of light used is decreased.
Q56. What is electrostatic shielding? Give one application.
Ans: Electrostatic Shielding: The process of protecting a certain region or instrument from the influence of external electric fields.
Application: It is achieved using a hollow conductor (Faraday cage) because the electric field inside a cavity is always zero. e.g., Sitting inside a car during lightning.
Q57. Calculate the work done in rotating an electric dipole in an external electric field.
Ans: The work done (W) in rotating a dipole from angle θ1 to θ2 in a uniform electric field E is:
W = pE (cosθ1 - cosθ2)
If rotated from stable equilibrium (θ=0), W = pE(1 - cosθ).
Q58. Define the mean (average) value of alternating current.
Ans: The average value of AC over a full cycle is zero. Hence, it is defined for a half-cycle.
The mean value of AC over a half-cycle is 2/π times the peak value (I0).
Iavg = 0.637 I0
Q59. Define isotopes, isobars, and isotones with examples.
Ans: Isotopes: Same atomic number (Z), different mass number (A). e.g., 1H1, 1H2.
Isobars: Same mass number (A), different atomic number (Z). e.g., 6C14, 7N14.
Isotones: Same number of neutrons. e.g., 1H3 and 2He4.
Q60. On what factors does the velocity of electromagnetic waves in a medium depend?
Ans: The velocity (v) of EM waves in a medium depends on the electric permittivity (ε) and magnetic permeability (μ) of that medium.
v = 1 / √(με)
In vacuum, v = c = 3 × 108 m/s.
