5. Write a note on American English.

English Answer: American English (AmE) is the variety of English spoken in the USA. It began to diverge from British English in the 17th century. The main differences lie in vocabulary, spelling, and pronunciation. For example, Americans use "color" instead of "colour," and "center" instead of "centre." Vocabulary differences include "elevator" (US) instead of "lift" (UK). Today, due to the global influence of Hollywood and technology, American English has become the most dominant form of English worldwide, standardizing many global communication norms.

हिंदी सारांश: अमेरिकी अंग्रेजी (AmE) अमेरिका में बोली जाती है। यह ब्रिटिश अंग्रेजी से शब्दावली और वर्तनी (Spelling) में भिन्न है। हॉलीवुड और तकनीक के प्रभाव के कारण आज यह दुनिया भर में सबसे अधिक प्रचलित रूप बन गया है।

6. Write a note on Indian English.

English Answer: Indian English is a distinct variety of English spoken in India, adapted to its social and cultural context. It is characterized by unique pronunciation and the inclusion of Indian words like "Guru" and "Lathi." Famous writers like R.K. It is a symbol of modernization and is essential for higher education and professional growth in the 21st century.

7. Write the summary of the poem: "Ode to Autumn".

English Answer: "Ode to Autumn" is a beautiful nature poem by John Keats. He describes autumn as the "season of mists and mellow fruitfulness." Autumn works with the sun to ripen fruits and fill flowers with honey. The poet personifies autumn as a farmer watching the harvest. In the final stanza, Keats highlights that autumn has its own music, like the singing of crickets and bleating of lambs. The poem teaches us to appreciate the beauty of maturity and the cycle of nature, showing that every season has its own unique charm and significance.

8. Write the summary of the poem: "The Soldier".

English Answer: "The Soldier" is a patriotic sonnet by Rupert Brooke. It expresses a soldier's deep love for his motherland, England. The poet says that if he dies in a foreign land, that spot will forever become part of England. He believes his body is made of English soil and his soul carries the gentleness learned from English friends. The poem is not about the horrors of war but about the glory of sacrifice and intense patriotism. It reflects the idea that a soldier’s sacrifice makes him immortal through the eternal memory of his country.

9. Write the summary of the prose piece: "Indian Civilization and Culture".

English Answer: In this essay, Mahatma Gandhi compares Indian civilization with Western civilization. He argues that Indian civilization is based on "Morality" and "Self-restraint," while Western civilization is based on "Materialism." Gandhi warns Indians not to copy the West blindly. He believes our ancestors preferred villages because cities were full of vice. He describes the mind as a "restless bird" that always wants more. The essence of Indian civilization lies in simple living and high thinking, which has successfully withstood the test of time and preserved our ancient values.

10. Write a note on the dialects of Middle English.

English Answer: During the Middle English period (1150–1500 AD), English existed in five major regional dialects: Northern, Southern, Kentish, East Midland, and West Midland. These dialects differed significantly in grammar and vocabulary. Among these, the East Midland dialect gained the most importance because it was spoken in London, Oxford, and Cambridge. It was the dialect used by Geoffrey Chaucer and later adopted for the printing press. This dialect eventually became the basis for Modern Standard English, unifying the linguistic diversity of the era into a single literary standard.

11. Write the summary of the prose piece: "A Child is Born".

English Answer: Germaine Greer explores cultural differences in childbirth between traditional and modern societies. In traditional societies, pregnancy is a collective family event where the woman receives immense support and rituals. For instance, she often goes to her mother’s house for the first birth. However, in modern Western societies, childbirth has become clinical and lonely, focusing more on hospital technology than the mother's emotional well-being. Greer emphasizes that while modern medicine has reduced mortality, it has also stripped childbirth of its social significance and emotional warmth found in traditional cultures.

12. Write the summary of the poem: "Now the Leaves are Falling Fast".

English Answer: W. H. Auden uses the imagery of falling leaves to symbolize old age and the inevitability of death. Just as trees become leafless in autumn, humans move rapidly towards the grave. The poet highlights the frustrations of modern life, where humans feel lonely and separated from true joy. He mentions "whispering neighbors" as messengers of death who snatch away our delights. The poem also touches upon the suffering of the poor. Ultimately, death is a cold, silent end, but the poet finds peace in nature's eternal beauty, suggesting that death is the final blessing for the traveler.

13. Write the summary of the prose piece: "I Have a Dream".

English Answer: Martin Luther King, Jr. delivered this speech to fight against racial discrimination in America. He highlights the pathetic condition of Negroes who were treated as exiles in their own land. He demands justice and equality, urging non-violent struggle. His famous "dream" is that his children will be judged by the "content of their character" and not the "color of their skin." He envisions a future where people of all races sit together in brotherhood. The speech remains a powerful symbol of hope and a call for civil rights and justice for oppressed people worldwide.

14. Write the summary of the poem: "Song of Myself".

English Answer: Walt Whitman celebrates himself as a representative of all humanity. He says, "I celebrate myself, and sing myself," but emphasizes that every atom of his blood is the same in every human being. He discards barriers of caste and religion, inviting everyone to enjoy life’s beauties and hazards. At 37, he is in perfect health and hopes to remain active until death. The poem is a powerful call for universal brotherhood and individualism, showing that all souls are interconnected through nature and common origin, transcending social and artificial boundaries.

15. Write a note on Modern English.

English Answer: Modern English began around 1500 AD. Two major events shaped this period: the introduction of the Printing Press and the Great Vowel Shift. Printing helped standardize spelling and grammar. Writers like Shakespeare and Milton enriched the language during the Renaissance. As the British Empire expanded, English borrowed words from many languages, evolving into a global lingua franca. Today, Modern English is characterized by simple grammar and a vast vocabulary. It is the dominant language of science, technology, and international communication, with variations like American and Indian English flourishing globally.

17. Write the summary of the poem: "Snake".

English Answer: D. H. Lawrence explores the conflict between human education and natural instinct. On a hot day, the poet finds a snake drinking at his water trough. He admires the snake as a "guest," but his "voice of education" tells him to kill it because it is poisonous. Torn between fear and admiration, he eventually throws a log at the snake. He immediately feels deep regret for his "paltry" act. He compares the snake to a "king in exile" and wishes he had acted with more dignity, realizing that human prejudices often prevent us from appreciating nature's majesty.

18. Write the summary of the prose piece: "The Earth".

English Answer: This story by H. E. Bates tells of a simple farmer, Johnson, and his son, Benjy. Benjy’s parents give him hens to keep him busy. While the parents are lazy and rely on fate, Benjy becomes cunning and successful in the poultry business. He eventually buys the land his father rented. After marrying Florence, he becomes cruel to his parents. Finally, he drives his old parents out of their home and leaves them on the street. The story is a grim reminder of the cold selfishness that can arise from materialism and how simple-mindedness can hide a sharp, calculating nature.

19. Write the summary of the prose piece: "A Pinch of Snuff".

English Answer: This is a humorous story about Nanukaka, who visits Delhi to meet ministers. Despite the narrator’s doubts, Nanukaka uses wit and lies to achieve his goals. He pretends to be a wealthy friend of an ambassador and later poses as the "Hereditary Astrologer of the Maharaja of Ninnore" to meet the Welfare Minister. His royal appearance and clever talk impress the minister, who eventually comes to visit him. The story is full of humor and highlights Nanukaka’s resourcefulness and his ability to navigate difficult situations with a pinch of snuff and a quick mind.
