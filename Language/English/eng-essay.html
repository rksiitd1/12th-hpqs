English (Class 12) - Top 12 Questions

Bihar Board | Essays, Summaries & Story of English | 5-Year Trend Analysis
1. Essay: Pollution & Climate Change

Frequency: Highest (2022, 2023, 2024, 2025)

Key Concept: This is the most repeated theme. Prepare points on Global Warming, Deforestation, Noise Pollution, and the "Clean India" mission.

Tip: If the topic is 'Pollution' or 'Climate Change', the content remains 80% same.

2. Essay: Discipline / Student Life

Frequency: Very High (2021, 2022, 2023)

Key Concept: Focus on punctuality, obedience, moral values, and the role of students in nation-building.

Tip: Also covers topics like "Value of Education" or "Importance of Sports".

3. Essay: My Aim in Life

Frequency: High (2022, 2023, 2025)

Key Concept: Define a specific goal (e.g., Doctor, Teacher) and explain your desire to serve society through it. Do not just write about earning money.

4. Note: English as a Global Language

Importance: The single most important question for the "Story of English" section.

Key Points: English is the "Window to the World," the language of science, technology, international trade, and diplomacy.

5. Note: Future of English in India

Importance: Tests relevance in modern India.

Key Points: It acts as a "Link Language" between North and South India. It is essential for higher education, job opportunities, and globalization.

6. Note: Old English vs Middle English

Importance: Historical context.

Key Points:

  • Old English (449-1066 AD): Dialect-heavy, difficult vocabulary.
  • Middle English (1150-1500 AD): Influence of French/Latin, Great Vowel Shift, invention of the Printing Press.
7. Summary: Indian Civilization & Culture

Author: Mahatma Gandhi

Theme: A comparison of Indian moral values vs. Western materialism. Gandhi argues that Indian civilization elevates the moral being, while Western civilization promotes self-indulgence.

8. Summary: Bharat is My Home

Author: Dr. Zakir Hussain

Theme: National Unity. In this speech, he pledges himself to the service of the "Totality of India's culture" and declares that the whole of Bharat is his home and its people are his family.

9. Summary: A Pinch of Snuff

Author: Manohar Malgaonkar

Theme: Wit and Humor. It narrates the story of the clever Nanukaka, who travels to Delhi and uses his presence of mind and lies to manage a meeting with the Welfare Minister.

10. Summary: Sweetest Love I Do Not Go

Poet: John Donne (Metaphysical Poet)

Theme: True Love. The poet consoles his beloved before a journey, arguing that true lovers are never parted. He compares his return to the sun's journey.

11. Summary: The Soldier

Poet: Rupert Brooke (War Poet)

Theme: Patriotism. A War Sonnet where the soldier expresses that if he dies in a foreign land, that piece of earth will become "forever England."

12. Summary: My Grandmother's House

Poet: Kamala Das

Theme: Nostalgia and Loss. The poet remembers the intense love she received in her grandmother's house, which is now silent, dark, and full of snakes after her grandmother's death.

Exam Strategy: How to Score 15/15

In Section B, Question 6, you must answer 3 out of 6 options. To guarantee full marks, attempt them in this order:

  1. Option 1: Match the Column (Prose) - Easiest 5 Marks.
  2. Option 2: Match the Column (Poetry) - Easiest 5 Marks.
  3. Option 3: Write ONE Summary (from this list) OR the 'Story of English' note.

Note: Avoid the Translation option if your grammar is weak, as marks are deducted for minor errors.
