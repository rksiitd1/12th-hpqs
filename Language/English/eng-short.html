ENGLISH - Short Answer Questions

Class 12 Bihar Board | 2 Marks Important Questions | Exam Special
1. How did Seibei's father react to the teacher's complaint?
Seibei’s father reacted extremely angrily to the teacher's complaint. He grabbed his son by the collar, scolded him for pursuing such an 'effeminate' hobby, and then proceeded to smash the precious, polished gourd to pieces with a hammer.
(सेईबेई के पिता ने गुस्से में प्रतिक्रिया दी। उन्होंने उसका कॉलर पकड़ा, उसे डांटा और उसके कीमती कद्दू को हथौड़े से तोड़ दिया।)
2. How does Mahatma Gandhi want his countrymen to deal with modern civilization?
Mahatma Gandhi wants his countrymen to cling to the old Indian civilization exactly as a child clings to its mother’s breast. He advises them to keep a safe distance from modern civilization because it is purely the worship of materialism and lacks moral values.
(गांधीजी चाहते हैं कि देशवासी पुरानी सभ्यता से वैसे ही चिपके रहें जैसे बच्चा माँ से। वे आधुनिक सभ्यता से दूर रहने की सलाह देते हैं।)
3. What are the 'trials and tribulations' that Martin Luther King Jr. talks about?
The phrase 'trials and tribulations' refers to the immense suffering faced by the Negroes in America. This includes police brutality, unjust imprisonment in narrow jail cells, and the gross racial persecution they endured while peacefully fighting for their civil rights.
(‘परीक्षण और क्लेश’ का अर्थ अमेरिका में अश्वेतों द्वारा झेले गए कष्ट, पुलिस की क्रूरता और जेल से है।)
4. What did the doctor advise Benjy’s parents to ensure his mental growth?
The doctor advised Benjy’s parents to give him some responsibility to keep his mind occupied and help his development. Specifically, he suggested that they let Benjy keep hens as a simple, manageable occupation to foster his understanding of work.
(डॉक्टर ने बेंजी को व्यस्त रखने के लिए कोई काम देने को कहा। उन्होंने मुर्गियां पालने का सुझाव दिया।)
5. What is the business of education according to Dr. Zakir Hussain?
According to Dr. Zakir Hussain, the business of education is to constantly renew the process of national culture and character building. He considers education to be the "prime instrument of national purpose" and essential for the quality of the nation.
(शिक्षा का कार्य राष्ट्रीय संस्कृति और चरित्र निर्माण को लगातार नवीनीकृत करना है।)
6. What do you mean by the term ‘free press’?
A ‘free press’ implies that the media is technically free from government direction, censorship, or control. It means newspapers are at liberty to publish news, criticize policies, and express public opinion without fear, except during extreme conditions like war.
(‘मुक्त प्रेस’ का अर्थ है कि मीडिया सरकारी सेंसरशिप से मुक्त है और बिना डर के समाचार छाप सकता है।)
7. Are there restrictions on the Press in time of war?
Yes, the freedom of the Press is restricted during times of war. The government imposes censorship to ensure that no secret information is leaked to the enemy and to maintain national security and public morale during the crisis.
(हाँ, युद्ध के समय प्रेस पर प्रतिबंध होता है ताकि दुश्मन को गुप्त जानकारी न मिले।)
8. How are the children of the joint family in Bangladesh looked after?
In Bangladesh, children in a joint family are not looked after by their parents alone. Instead, they are cared for collectively by the entire family, including aunts, grandmothers, and other relatives, creating a shared environment of love and responsibility.
(बांग्लादेश में संयुक्त परिवार के बच्चों की देखभाल पूरा परिवार मिलकर करता है, न कि सिर्फ माता-पिता।)
9. What is the reward of pregnancy for a young Sylheti woman?
The reward for a young Sylheti woman is that she gets the privilege of going to her mother’s house for the delivery. There, she is treated with great care like a princess, receives immense love, and is gifted new clothes and accessories.
(उसे डिलीवरी के लिए मायके जाने को मिलता है, जहाँ उसे राजकुमारी जैसा प्यार और सम्मान मिलता है।)
10. What does Pearl S. Buck mean by ‘Religion is ever present in Indian life’?
She means that religion influences every single aspect of life in India, from birth ceremonies to death rituals. Indians do not separate their daily actions, social customs, or even politics from their deep-rooted religious and spiritual beliefs.
(उनका अर्थ है कि भारत में धर्म जीवन के हर पहलू में मौजूद है, जन्म से मृत्यु तक।)
11. Why were the intellectuals in India restless and embittered?
The intellectuals were restless and embittered because, despite being highly educated, they were unemployed and had no suitable jobs. They felt suffocated and enslaved by British rule and saw no hopeful future for their country under colonial administration.
(वे शिक्षित होकर भी बेरोजगार थे और ब्रिटिश शासन में खुद को गुलाम महसूस करते थे।)
12. Why did the writer not mind her host eating in the opposite corner?
The writer did not mind because she understood and respected it as a local religious custom. She knew that due to caste purity rules, her host could not eat in the same space as a "white" outsider, so she accepted it gracefully.
(वह जानती थी कि यह एक धार्मिक रिवाज है और जाति के नियमों के कारण वे साथ नहीं खा सकते।)
13. How is Lomov greeted by Choobookov?
Choobookov greets Lomov with extreme warmth and happiness. He embraces and kisses him, calls him affectionate names like "my angel" and "my treasure," and expresses great joy, hoping that Lomov has finally come to propose marriage to his daughter.
(चुबुकोव उसका स्वागत गले लगाकर और चूमकर करता है, यह उम्मीद करते हुए कि वह रिश्ता लेकर आया है।)
14. What made Seibei's heart beat faster?
Seibei's heart began to beat unnaturally fast when he spotted a small, perfectly symmetrical gourd about five inches long at an old woman’s stall. Its unique shape and flawless beauty excited him more than any other gourd he had seen.
(पाँच इंच का एक सुडौल कद्दू देखकर उसका दिल तेजी से धड़कने लगा।)
15. What is the 'sheet anchor of our hope' according to Gandhi?
According to Mahatma Gandhi, the 'sheet anchor of our hope' lies in continuing the active, simple life of the villages and the use of the spinning wheel, rather than chasing the destructive material progress and industrialization of the cities.
(हमारी आशा का आधार शहरों की प्रगति में नहीं, बल्कि गाँवों के जीवन और चरखे में है।)
16. Why is Macavity called the 'Hidden Paw'?
Macavity is called the 'Hidden Paw' because he is a master criminal who executes his plans perfectly but is never found at the scene of the crime. He leaves no pawprints or evidence, making it impossible for Scotland Yard to catch him.
(क्योंकि वह अपराध करके गायब हो जाता है और कभी भी घटनास्थल पर नहीं मिलता।)
17. Who was Nanukaka and why did he come to Delhi?
Nanukaka was the narrator’s maternal uncle (mother's brother). He travelled to Delhi specifically to meet a Welfare Minister regarding some personal business related to a zamindari issue, which he intended to resolve using his wit and influence.
(नानुकाका कथाकार के मामा थे। वे किसी मंत्री से मिलने दिल्ली आए थे।)
18. Who are the 'ancestors' referred to by Gandhi?
The 'ancestors' refer to our Indian forefathers who lived simple lives in villages. They followed the path of duty and morality, avoided the vices of city life, and were satisfied with their small, self-reliant communities and traditional professions.
(वे हमारे पूर्वज थे जो गाँवों में रहते थे और नैतिकता का पालन करते थे।)
19. What do free people take for granted?
People living in a democracy often take their freedom of speech and the freedom of the press for granted. They do not fully realize how precious these rights are until they are threatened or suppressed during a national crisis or war.
(स्वतंत्र लोग अक्सर अपनी बोलने की आजादी और प्रेस की स्वतंत्रता को हल्के में लेते हैं।)
20. In what sense does the fire forget its dead?
The fire is said to 'forget its dead' when it fails to perform its duty of burning the dead body completely. It leaves some limbs or bones half-burnt or charred, which the poet considers an immoral act of negligence by the fire.
(आग अपने मृतकों को तब भूल जाती है जब वह शरीर को पूरी तरह जलाने में विफल रहती है।)
21. How are autumn and summer related to spring?
They are related as interconnected parts of the natural cycle. Spring represents birth and youth, Summer represents growth and maturity, and Autumn represents the climax of fruition and old age, thus completing the life cycle that began with Spring.
(वसंत जन्म है, ग्रीष्म परिपक्वता है, और शरद ऋतु चक्र को पूरा करती है।)
22. Was the Snake conscious of the poet's presence?
No, initially the snake did not seem conscious of the poet’s presence at all. It continued to drink water peacefully and looked around vaguely with glassy eyes, just like cattle do, without noticing the poet standing in the shadows nearby.
(नहीं, शुरुआत में सांप को कवि की उपस्थिति का आभास नहीं था।)
23. What is the belief prevailing in Sicily about a snake?
In Sicily, there is a strong superstition regarding snakes. It is believed that black snakes are innocent and harmless, whereas the gold or yellow-coloured snakes are highly venomous and must be killed immediately to prevent danger.
(काले सांप निर्दोष माने जाते हैं, जबकि सुनहरे सांप जहरीले होते हैं।)
24. How did the snake drink water?
The snake drank water very slowly and rhythmically. It rested its throat upon the stone bottom and sipped the water into its slack, long body with its straight gums, pausing occasionally to look around just like cattle do when drinking.
(सांप ने मवेशियों की तरह धीरे-धीरे और रुक-रुक कर पानी पिया।)
25. Why did the speaker like the snake?
The speaker liked the snake because it appeared like a guest who had come to his water trough to quench its thirst. He was fascinated by its majestic beauty, its peaceful movement, and the quiet dignity with which it drank the water.
(वह एक मेहमान जैसा लग रहा था। कवि उसकी शाही सुंदरता से प्रभावित था।)
26. What happens in autumn?
In autumn, the sun becomes mild and works with the season to load the vines with grapes. Fruits ripen to their core, flowers bloom for the bees, and the entire landscape is filled with "mists and mellow fruitfulness" before winter arrives.
(शरद ऋतु में फल पकते हैं और मधुमक्खियों के लिए फूल खिलते हैं।)
27. What does the speaker in 'Song of Myself' observe in summer?
In the poem 'Song of Myself', the speaker observes a "spear of summer grass" while resting on the ground. This symbolizes his deep connection with nature, as he spends time observing even the smallest and simplest details of the natural world.
(वक्ता गर्मियों में "घास के एक तिनके" का निरीक्षण करता है।)
28. What does Walt Whitman want to do with creeds?
Walt Whitman wants to set aside all creeds (religious beliefs) and rigid schools of thought "in abeyance." He prefers to believe in the raw power of Nature and humanity, wishing to speak out freely without being checked by traditional religious rules.
(वह सभी धार्मिक मान्यताओं को एक तरफ रखकर प्रकृति में विश्वास करना चाहता है।)
29. Who are the 'whispering neighbours'?
The phrase 'whispering neighbours' is a metaphor for the agents or messengers of death. They are the forces that quietly come and snatch people away from their happy, living bodies, leaving them lifeless and separating them from their loved ones.
(‘कानाफूसी करने वाले पड़ोसी’ मृत्यु के दूतों को कहा गया है।)
30. How do sighing and weeping affect the speaker?
The speaker feels physically hurt by his beloved's grief. He says that when she sighs, she sighs his "soul away," and when she weeps, his "life-blood" decays. This exaggeration shows that her pain causes him actual physical and emotional suffering.
(उसकी आहों से वक्ता की 'आत्मा' निकलती है और रोने से उसका 'रक्त' क्षय होता है।)
31. If he dies, how would the soldier like to be remembered?
The soldier wishes to be remembered as a loyal piece of England left in a foreign land. He hopes that the specific spot of earth where he is buried will be forever considered "England," representing his eternal bond with his motherland.
(वह चाहता है कि उसे विदेशी भूमि में इंग्लैंड के एक अंश के रूप में याद किया जाए।)
32. Is the speaker in ‘The Soldier’ afraid of death?
No, the speaker is absolutely not afraid of death. He is a brave patriot who is willing to sacrifice his life for his country, England. He finds peace and honor in the idea of dying on the battlefield for his nation.
(नहीं, वह नहीं डरता। वह देश के लिए जान देने को तैयार है।)
33. How do we complete our last journey to the grave?
We complete our last journey to the grave in utter loneliness. The "cold, impassive hands" of death freeze us, and we are separated from all our friends and loved ones as we move towards the inevitable end of our life's journey.
(हम कब्र तक अपनी अंतिम यात्रा अकेलेपन में पूरी करते हैं।)
34. What was the role of fire in our development?
The discovery of fire was a major milestone in human development. It provided early humans with warmth, protection from wild animals, and the ability to cook food, which eventually led to a more settled and civilized way of life.
(आग ने भोजन पकाने, गर्म रहने और सुरक्षा में मदद की।)
35. Where did the speaker in 'My Grandmother's House' receive love?
The speaker received immense, unconditional love in her grandmother's house during her childhood. She remembers that house as a sanctuary of safety and affection, a place she deeply misses now that she has lost her grandmother and the house is silent.
(उसे बचपन में अपनी दादी के घर में बहुत प्यार मिला था।)
36. What does the poet mean by 'an epitaph'?
An 'epitaph' refers to a short text or inscription written on a tombstone in memory of a deceased person. In the poem, the poet uses it to describe the beauty and the transient nature of the lady lying in the grave.
(एपितेफ वह लेख है जो कब्र के पत्थर पर लिखा जाता है।)
37. Who are the 'travellers' in 'Now the Leaves are Falling Fast'?
The 'travellers' are a metaphor for the old people who are nearing the end of their life's journey. They are moving towards death, described as their "last distress," and are often left lonely as they approach the grave.
(यात्री वे बूढ़े लोग हैं जो मृत्यु की ओर बढ़ रहे हैं।)
38. Why does the poet call Macavity a 'criminal'?
The poet calls Macavity a criminal because he openly defies all laws, including the law of gravity. He steals food, breaks greenhouses, and commits mischief, yet he is never caught by Scotland Yard because he is too clever and elusive.
(क्योंकि वह कानून तोड़ता है, चोरी करता है और कभी पकड़ा नहीं जाता।)
39. What was the impact of the Norman Conquest on vocabulary?
The Norman Conquest of 1066 had a massive impact on English vocabulary. It introduced thousands of French words related to administration, law, military, fashion, and art, which enriched the language and created a blend of Old English and French terms.
(नॉर्मन विजय ने अंग्रेजी में हजारों फ्रेंच शब्द जोड़े।)
40. Write a note on English as a Global Language.
English is considered a global language because it is spoken and understood in almost every country. It serves as the primary language for international trade, science, technology, diplomacy, and the internet, effectively linking people from different cultures across the world.
(अंग्रेजी एक वैश्विक भाषा है क्योंकि यह दुनिया भर में व्यापार और विज्ञान की भाषा है।)
41. Name four countries where English is used as a second language.
There are many countries where English plays a key role as a second language. Four prominent examples include **India, Singapore, Nigeria, and Malaysia**. In these nations, English is widely used for official communication, education, and administrative purposes.
(भारत, सिंगापुर, नाइजीरिया और मलेशिया।)
42. When did the Great Vowel Shift take place?
The Great Vowel Shift took place during the Middle English period, roughly spanning from **1400 to 1700**. It was a major linguistic event that permanently changed the pronunciation of long vowels in English, making them sound different from how they are spelled.
(यह मध्य अंग्रेजी काल के दौरान, 1400 और 1700 के बीच हुआ था।)
43. What standardized English spelling and pronunciation?
Two major factors standardized English: the invention of the **Printing Press** by William Caxton in 1476, which required consistent spelling for books, and the publication of **Dr. Samuel Johnson’s Dictionary** in 1755, which fixed spelling and grammar rules.
(प्रिंटिंग प्रेस और डॉ. जॉनसन की डिक्शनरी ने मुख्य भूमिका निभाई।)
44. What did 18th-century grammarians think about their role?
The 18th-century grammarians believed their primary role was to "fix" the English language. They wanted to establish strict rules for correct usage, standardize grammar, and prevent the language from changing any further, an approach known as Prescriptivism.
(वे भाषा को "ठीक" करना और नियम बनाना चाहते थे।)
45. Who were known as the University Wits?
The University Wits were a group of brilliant late 16th-century playwrights who were educated at Oxford or Cambridge. This group included famous figures like **Christopher Marlowe, Robert Greene, Thomas Nash,** and **John Lyly**, who paved the way for Shakespeare.
(वे 16वीं सदी के नाटककार थे जो ऑक्सफोर्ड या कैम्ब्रिज से पढ़े थे।)
46. Who is called the 'Father of the Modern Novel’?
**Samuel Richardson** is most often cited as the Father of the Modern Novel for his epistolary work *Pamela*. However, **Henry Fielding** is also frequently credited with this title for his significant contributions to the development of the English novel form.
(सैमुअल रिचर्डसन को अक्सर आधुनिक उपन्यास का जनक कहा जाता है।)
47. What is the dramatic structure of a tragedy?
The standard dramatic structure of a tragedy consists of five parts: **Exposition** (Introduction), **Complication** (Rising Action), **Crisis** (Climax), **Denouement** (Falling Action), and finally the **Catastrophe** (Tragic End), which usually involves the death of the protagonist.
(इसमें शामिल हैं: प्रस्तावना, जटिलता, संकट, और दुखद अंत।)
48. Name three areas of English where rapid changes are noticed.
Rapid changes in English have been noticed in: **(1) Vocabulary**, with new words from technology; **(2) Spelling**, influenced by internet slang and SMS language; and **(3) Pronunciation**, which is evolving due to globalization and American media influence.
(शब्दावली, वर्तनी और उच्चारण में तेजी से बदलाव देखे गए हैं।)
49. What made English ‘the most gloriously, empire language’?
It was the British colonization of vast parts of the world that spread English globally. Additionally, its unique ability to absorb and adapt words from foreign languages made it a rich and "glorious empire language" that united diverse people.
(उपनिवेशवाद ने इसे फैलाया और अन्य भाषाओं के शब्दों को अपनाने की क्षमता ने इसे महान बनाया।)
50. Write a note on development of Indian poetry in English.
Indian poetry in English began in the 19th century with pioneers like **Henry Derozio** and **Toru Dutt**. It flourished with giants like **Tagore** and **Sri Aurobindo**, and later evolved into modern styles with poets like **Nissim Ezekiel** and **Kamala Das**.
(यह 19वीं सदी में शुरू हुई और टैगोर तथा आधुनिक कवियों ने इसे आगे बढ़ाया।)
51. Name the three periods of the English language.
The history of the English language is divided into three main periods: **Old English** (AD 449 – AD 1066), **Middle English** (AD 1150 – AD 1500), and **Modern English**, which covers the period from AD 1500 to the present day.
(ओल्ड इंग्लिश, मिडिल इंग्लिश और मॉडर्न इंग्लिश।)
52. Name two major works of William Shakespeare.
William Shakespeare wrote many famous plays. Two of his major works include the tragedy **Hamlet** and the comedy **As You Like It**. Other globally renowned plays include *Macbeth*, *Othello*, and *Romeo and Juliet*.
(हैमलेट और एज यू लाइक इट।)
53. What is the difference between Old and Middle English?
The main difference is that Old English was highly inflected with complex grammar and German roots. In contrast, Middle English was simpler, lost many grammatical inflections, and was heavily influenced by French vocabulary following the Norman Conquest.
(ओल्ड इंग्लिश जटिल थी; मिडिल इंग्लिश सरल और फ्रेंच से प्रभावित थी।)
54. Why is English called a 'window to the world'?
English is called a 'window to the world' because it serves as the primary medium for global communication. It allows us to access international news, scientific research, technology, and literature, connecting us to the wider world outside our own country.
(यह हमें वैश्विक ज्ञान और समाचार से जोड़ती है।)
55. What constitutes 'civilization' in the real sense?
In the real sense, civilization does not mean the multiplication of wants or material wealth. Instead, it constitutes the deliberate and voluntary restriction of wants. It is about self-control, moral living, and finding happiness in simplicity.
(सभ्यता का अर्थ है इच्छाओं पर जानबूझकर प्रतिबंध लगाना।)
56. What did Seibei do with the gourds after buying them?
After buying the gourds, Seibei would take them home and treat them with great care. He would clean them with sake (alcohol) to remove unpleasant smells and then polish them for hours to enhance their shape and color.
(वह उन्हें घर ले जाकर साके से साफ करता और चमकाता था।)
57. How did Nanukaka impress the Minister?
Nanukaka impressed the Minister by using his wit and appearance. He arrived in a grand, chauffeur-driven car and confidently posed as the hereditary astrologer to the Maharaja of Ninnores, which immediately gained the Minister's respect and attention.
(वह एक शानदार कार में पहुँचा और खुद को शाही ज्योतिषी बताया।)
58. Why was the poet waiting for the snake?

59. What is the message of 'Sweetest Love I Do Not Go'?
The core message of the poem is that true love transcends physical separation. The poet assures his beloved that he will return quickly, and that those who truly love each other are connected by their souls and can never be parted.
(सच्चा प्यार शारीरिक दूरी से परे होता है।)
60. What is the immediate impact of poverty in the medical field?
The immediate impact of poverty is that traditional societies cannot afford modern hospital equipment or drugs. Consequently, childbirth remains a traditional, home-based affair managed by family members, with fewer medical interventions but often more emotional support.
